## 3. Vocabulary
- **Unified Inbox**: The central feed that combines Email, SMS, Facebook Messenger, Instagram DMs, Google Business Profile (GBP) Chat, and WhatsApp.
- **Manual Actions**: A special "To-Do List" created by a Robot. The automation pauses and waits for YOU to do the task (like a call) before it continues.
- **Templates**: Pre-written "cheat codes" for common replies (e.g., "Here is the pricing PDF").
- **Snippets**: Short blocks of text (like a signature or a disclaimer) you can insert instantly.
- **Internal Notes**: Private comments you write *inside* a conversation thread that the client cannot see (e.g., "@Manager, this lead is asking for a refund").

## 4. Deep Dive: The Universal Smartphone (Orientation)

### The "One Screen" Concept
The most powerful feature of the Conversations tab is that **it doesn't care where the message came from**.
Whether a lead sends an SMS ("U up?") or a formal Email ("Please find attached..."), it appears in the same timeline.
*   **You reply to SMS**, and it sends as a text.
*   **You reply to Email**, and it sends as an email.
*   **You can switch channels** mid-conversation. If a lead emails you, you can manually toggle to SMS (little phone icon) to send them a quick text notification: *"Hey, just sent you the proposal via email!"*

## 5. Manual Actions: The Relay Race Metaphor

Understanding "Manual Actions" is crucial because it's where Humans and Robots work together. Think of it like a **Relay Race**.

### Phase 1: The Robot Runs (Automation)
The workflow starts via a trigger (e.g., "New Lead Form Submitted"). The robot runs the first lap:
1.  It creates the contact.
2.  It sends a "Welcome" email.
3.  It tags the lead "New".

### Phase 2: The Handoff (The Pause)
Suddenly, the Robot hits a step called **"Manual Call"**.
The Robot *cannot* make a human phone call. So, it stops running. It stands perfectly still, holding the **Baton**.
It adds the lead to a list called **Manual Actions** and waits.
*   *If you verify this workflow now, the lead will look "stuck" at this step.*

### Phase 3: The Human Sprint (You)
You log in and go to **Conversations > Manual Actions**.
You see "5 Calls Pending". You click **"Let's Start"**.
The system acts as your coach:
1.  It dials the first number.
2.  You talk to the lead.
3.  You select an outcome: "No Answer", "Voicemail", or "Connected".
4.  The system *immediately* dials the next number.
You are running your lap.

### Phase 4: The Handoff Back (Completion)
Once you finish the call and mark the task as **"Complete"**, you hand the **Baton** back to the Robot.
The Robot immediately starts running again! It moves to the next step (e.g., "Send Follow-up SMS").

> **ðŸ›‘ CRITICAL PITFALL:** If you make the call *outside* of the Manual Actions tab (e.g., just dialing from the contact view), the Robot never gets the baton back. It waits handling the baton forever. The lead never gets the follow-up SMS. **Always use the Manual Actions tab.**

## 6. Speed Tools: Templates & Filters

### Templates (The Chef's Mise-en-place)
A professional chef doesn't chop an onion every time they make an omelet. They have a bowl of chopped onions ready to grab.
**Templates** are your pre-chopped onions.

**How to create one:**
1.  Go to **Conversations > Templates**.
2.  Click **"Add Template"** > **"Text"**.
3.  Name it: `FAQs - Directions`.
4.  Body: *"Hi {{contact.first_name}}, our office is located at 123 Main St, right behind the Starbucks. Here is a Google Maps link: [Link]. See you soon!"*
5.  Save.

**How to use it:**
When a client asks "Where are you?", you do NOT type.
1.  Click the **"Use Template"** icon (paper w/ fold).
2.  Select `FAQs - Directions`.
3.  Click Send.
*Time saved: 45 seconds per lead.*

### The Triage Protocol (Inbox Zero)
Your inbox will fill up fast. You need a system to clear it.
1.  **Filter by "Unread":** This is your specific To-Do list. Ignor everything else.
2.  **Scan for "Urgent":** Look for keywords like "Buy", "Ready", "Problem", "Refund".
3.  **The 2-Minute Rule:**
    *   If you can answer in < 2 mins (using a Template), do it now.
    *   If it needs research, add an **Internal Note** tagging your manager (@Manager) and mark it "Starred".
4.  **Archive:** Once you reply, click the **Folder/Archive** icon. This hides the thread from the "Unread" view. It DOES NOT delete it. It just files it away so you can focus on the next fire.

## 7. Lab Assignment: "The Triage Drill"

**Scenario:**
You log in on Monday morning. The inbox shows "3 Unread".
1.  **Lead A (SMS):** "I'm standing outside the gate, what's the code??" (Sent 2 mins ago)
2.  **Lead B (Email):** "Hi, I'd like to request a PDF of your pricing packages for Q4." (Sent 1 hour ago)
3.  **Lead C (FB Messenger):** "Do you guys have availability tomorrow?" (Sent 3 hours ago)

**Your Mission:**
Write down your exact sequence of actions.

**Solution Key:**
1.  **Lead A First (Urgent/SMS):** Reply *immediately* with the code. This is an "On Fire" situation.
2.  **Lead C Second (Medium/Chat):** Check the **Calendar** tab. Reply: *"Hi! Yes we have slots at 2pm and 4pm. Which works?"*
3.  **Lead B Third (Slow/Email):** This requires a document.
    *   Click the **Paperclip** icon.
    *   Attach `Pricing_Q4.pdf`.
    *   Insert Template: `Email - Pricing Delivery`.
    *   Send.
4.  **Final Action:** Select All -> **Mark as Read/Archive**.
*The inbox should now be empty.*
